Paddy

Water Level: Sandy Soil: Requires 6-10 inches of water due to low retention.

Peaty Soil: Needs 4-6 inches of water, as it holds moisture

Saline Soil: Requires 4-8 inches of water, with periodic flushing to prevent salt buildup.

Frequency: Maintain standing water during the growing period.

Additional Info: Ensure proper drainage during the maturation stage to avoid waterlogging.

Cotton

Water Level:Sandy Soil: Needs 8-12 inches of water, due to quick drainage

Peaty Soil: Requires 6-8 inches of water, offering good moisture retention

Saline Soil: Needs 6-10 inches of water, with periodic flushing.

Frequency: Every 15 days in dry conditions.

Additional Info: Critical stages for irrigation include flowering and boll formation.

Banana

Water Level: Sandy Soil: Needs 12-16 inches of water, as it drains quickly.

Peaty Soil: Requires 8-12 inches of water, retaining moisture well

Saline Soil: Needs 8-12 inches of water, with regular flushing to prevent salt buildup and maintain healthy banana growth.

Frequency: Weekly during dry periods or as needed to maintain soil moisture.

Additional Info: Avoid water stagnation around the roots to prevent diseases.
